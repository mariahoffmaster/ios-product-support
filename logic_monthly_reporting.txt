OVERVIEW
--------
Daily asset funding is the process by which newly originated HEAs (Home Equity Agreements)
are funded and wires are sent to title companies on behalf of customers. Funding comes from
two sources that together make up the Total Investment Payment:

  1. TCB Draw (90%) — an advance from the Texas Capital Bank (TCB) warehouse line to the
     UF2 Funding Account.
  2. Unlock Haircut (10%) — Unlock's own funds, moved from the UPS balance sheet account
     (x0292) into the UF2 Funding Account on funding day.

The UF2 Draw Request is Unlock's formal request to TCB to fund the advance portion.
The net_wire_amt sent to title companies equals the sum of investment_payment for all
assets being funded that day.


KEY ACCOUNTS
------------
- UPS — Unlock balance sheet account at JPMorgan Chase (x0292). Source of the 10% haircut.
- UF2 Funding Account — Texas Capital Bank account (x3836). Receives both the 90% TCB
  advance and the 10% haircut. Wires to title companies go out from here.
- UF2 Collections Account — TCB account (x3844). Used for incoming settlement wires,
  margin calls, and cancellation fund receipts.
- TCB Warehouse Line — Texas Capital Bank credit facility providing 90% of daily investment
  payments. Kevin Nerney is the TCB account admin.

KEY TERMINOLOGY
---------------
- investment_payment — The full dollar amount Unlock pays for an HEA. Sum of all
  investment_payments = net_wire_amt for the day.
- net_wire_amt — The total wire amount sent to title companies. Equals sum of
  investment_payment for all assets being funded.
- oface — Original face value of the investment (same as investment_payment at origination).
- value_date — The date wires are sent to title companies (the funding date).
- effective_date — The deal effective date. Assets flagged red in BulkFunding if they
  exceed 10 days past effective date (Column K check).
- pipeline_label / customer_pipeline_label_max — Identifies the investor account the
  asset belongs to.
- Unlock Haircut — The 10% portion of investment_payment funded by Unlock from UPS.
- TCB Advance / Draw — The 90% portion funded by Texas Capital Bank.
- UF2 Draw Request — Formal borrowing base certification sent to TCB requesting the advance.
- Draw Confirmation — DocuSign form returned by TCB; Kevin Nerney signs it to authorize release.
- Pending Escrow Disbursement — MAX sub-stage for assets ready to be funded.
- Pending Recording — MAX sub-stage after funding wires are sent.
- CET — ClearEdge Title. Primary title company receiving funding wires.
- FA — First American Title. Secondary title company.
- FA Hawaii — First American Hawaii. Occasional deals only.
- Suntera — Receives Investment Closing Statement (ICS) after MAX button is clicked.
- ICS — Investment Closing Statement sent to Suntera upon funding.


DAY 1 WORKFLOW (~3:30 PM EST the day before funding)

   business date.

   - After new assets are pulled, copy down or clear formulas/data from prior date.
   - If static data is missing (state, address), flag to Mike Dean in #badger-ops.
   - Verify accuracy against the count and total investment payment sent to post closing.
   - NOTE: Checks in rows 33–36 read FALSE until Badger Money button is clicked.
   - After completing checks, copy asset list into #post_closing message. Pull columns B–D
     from row 39 (asset ID, customer name/address, investment payment) and reply to thread.

5. Press Haircut Button.
   - Calculates 10% Unlock haircut amount.

   - Creates journal entries in Badger and records them in the Trades table.

8. Click Email Buttons (notifies TCB and title companies).

DAY 2 WORKFLOW — HAIRCUT TRANSFER (~10:30 AM EST on funding date)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Overview: Move 10% haircut from UPS account (x0292) into UF2 Funding Account. Once
TCB draw hits UF2, initiate wires to title companies.

1. Open BulkFunding Template. On Lookup tab, update Value Date (Cell A2) to =TODAY().

2. Log into TCB website (securelogin.texascapitalbank.com) → Activity Center.

3. Locate previous Haircut funds transfer from UPS → UF2. Copy it (Ellipses ⋯ → Copy).

4. Confirm transfer direction: From UPS → To UF2.

5. Delete prior amount; enter today's haircut amount.

6. Confirm Transfer Date = Today.

7. Update Memo: "Unlock UF2 Haircut YYYY.MM.DD".

8. Click Transfer Funds. No approval required for internal transfers.


DAY 2 — TITLE COMPANY WIRES (~11:30 AM EST)
--------------------------------------------
Trigger: TCB wires 90% advance to UF2 Funding Account (x3836). Notification via email
or Slack. If TCB draw does not hit by 11am, inform post closing.

Step 1: Draft Wires (in TCB portal)
  - Go to Money Movement → ACH/Wire Transfers.
  - Select template for correct title company. Ellipses ⋯ → Pay.
  - Set Process Date = Today, Amount = Net Wire Amount.
  - Message to Beneficiary format: "Unlock UF2 Funding YYYY.MM.D: [N] HEAs"
  - Click Draft. Repeat for both title companies + FA Hawaii if applicable.

Step 2: Update BulkFunding Template + Send Emails
  - Press Unlock Logo Button.
  - Generates 2 emails: one to WSFS UF2 split tape, one internal to Brian & Kevin.
  - Include Omni link in the internal email.

Step 3: Request Wire Approvals in #cash_management Slack Channel
  - Deadline for approvals: 1:30 PM EST. Escalate to Jagat Shah if not approved by 1 PM.
  - Wires require dual approval. Post message with asset counts, investment payments,
    and total for each title company. Include Omni link.
  - Required approvers: Jagat Shah, Kevin Nerney, Brian Kay.

Step 4: Click MAX Button (after wires are approved)
  - DO NOT click anything or use other applications until all emails are sent.
  - This button:
    - Attaches and sends Investment Closing Statement (ICS) to Suntera.
    - Flips MAX SubStage to Pending Recording.
    - Updates Fundings_New status to Funded.
  - "FLiPpeD" confirmation = process complete.


SPECIAL CASES
-------------
Exception Requests:
  If an exception is needed, include it in the TCB email sent on Day 1. Exceptions can
  cause next-day funding delays and change-of-docs situations.

MAX Button Interruption:
  If the MAX button is interrupted mid-run, determine which assets were flipped (ICS sent)
  vs. which were not. On the Email tab in BulkFunding, clear (DO NOT delete) deals that
  already flipped. Re-paste only the unflipped deals and re-run.


FLOW OF FUNDS SUMMARY (from TCB Funding Flow diagrams)
-------------------------------------------------------
Closing Flow:
  UPS (originator) → pledges Participation Certificate (PC) to UF2 (LLC) →
  UF2 pledges PC to TCB → TCB issues Participation Interest (PI).

Draw Flow:
  UPS generates tape (Request Date -1 BD) → tape sent to TCB →
  Unlock sends haircut (10%) to TCB →
  TCB advances 90% to UF2 → UF2 wires to CET on Purchase Date →
  TCB sends Draw Amount Certification (borrowing base) for Unlock to sign and return.

Funding Flow of Funds Example:
  Total Investment Payment: $100,000
  TCB Advance (90%):        $90,000 → UF2
  Unlock Haircut (10%):     $10,000 → UF2
  Combined wire to CET:     $100,000
  From CET to consumer (net): investment_payment - origination_fee - appraisal - payoffs
  Origination Fee:          $4,900 (included in investment_payment, reimbursed via fee)
  Appraisal Cost:           $395

Certification Flow:
  UPS sends tape to TCB (Pledge Date) → TCB delivers asset files electronically to WSFS
  within 2 BD of Pledge Date → WSFS reviews and issues Exchange Certificate
  (2 BD after receiving files + 2 BD for exceptions) → physical files delivered post-recording.
