Responsible Department: Investment Operations

OVERVIEW
--------
A settlement occurs when a homeowner (customer) pays off or exits their HEA. Settlement
funds are wired to the UF2 Collections account (x3844) at Texas Capital Bank (TCB).
Investment Operations monitors the #settlement_queue Slack channel for incoming wire
notifications, then processes each settlement through the Settlement Quick Publisher tool
(SettlementQuickPublish - JPM_Formatted.xlsm).

After processing, a Suntera notification email is sent, MAX is updated, and journal entries
are inserted into the dbo.Trades table. Monthly, settlements are reconciled and fees
are calculated per investor agreement.


KEY ACCOUNTS
------------
- UF2 Collections Account — TCB x3844. Receives all incoming settlement wires.
- JPM Omnibus Collections Account — JPMorgan Chase. Used for customer refund checks.
- JPM UAM Account — Pre-funded JPMorgan account used to send refund checks.
- Investor Trust Holdings Accounts — Where exercise value + settlement fee + lien release
  fee are swept within 2 BD of settlement receipt.


KEY SYSTEMS AND TOOLS
---------------------
- Settlement Quick Publisher — Excel file at:
  Asset Management - Documents\05 - Initiatives\Settlement Quick Publisher\
  SettlementQuickPublish - JPM_Formatted.xlsm
  Contains: Paintbrush Button (clear), Wire Transfer Button, Email Man Button,
  Wire-Trades Tab (for Slack notification copy), Month-of tabs (one per month).
- Settlement Calculator — Flags underpayments. Do not process until full amount received.
- Badger — Used to look up Asset ID by other identifiers when missing from notification.
- SQL client (TOAD or SSMS) — Used for TCB bank transaction queries and journal inserts.


KEY TABLES AND STORED PROCEDURES
---------------------------------
- dbo.Trades — Receives journal entries for each settlement. Fields: ASSET_ID, OFACE,
  ACCOUNT_ID, BUY_SELL, COUNTERPARTY_ID, TRADE_DATE, SETTLE_DATE, PRICE.
- dbo.SettlementTrans — Receives outgoing BANKTRANS IDs from Collections transfers.
  Populated via =SQL.Insert("SettlementTrans",) function from UnlockToolkit.
- dbo.Fundings_New — Settlement cancellations update fundingstatus_id to 4 (Cancelled).
- dbo.REO — Updated with reo_settlement_date, reo_net_proceeds, reo_home_sale_price
  for REO (Real Estate Owned) disposition settlements.
- EXEC GetTCBBankTrans [amount] — Stored proc to retrieve TCB Bank Transaction IDs
  (tcbinv_id / tcb_banktrans_id) by dollar amount. Used for underpayment processing.
- EXEC dbo.ResetSettlement '[asset_id]' — Deletes/cancels a previously processed settlement.
- EXEC ProcessAssetFailedToClose '[asset_id]' — Standard funded asset cancellation.
- EXEC ProcessAssetFailedToClose '[asset_id]', 1 — Cancellation for Right of Rescission
  (sets uwissue_id = 6).


KEY TERMINOLOGY
---------------
- settlement_type — The type of settlement (e.g., standard sale, Unlock Refinance, REO).
- settlement_reason — Reason for the settlement.
- settlement_account — Account receiving settlement proceeds.
- net_settlement_payment — The net amount received in a settlement.
- final_disposition_date — Date the deal was finally settled/disposed.
- BANKTRANS_ID / tcbinv_id — TCB Bank Transaction ID. Links bank wire to Badger records.
  Required for the Month-of Settlement tab and Badger Cash Transactions entry.
- Unlock Settlement Fee — $500 per settlement (waived for Unlock Refinances).
- Lien Release Fee — $75 per settlement.
- Exercise Value — The investor's share of the settlement proceeds per the HEA terms.
- Unlock Refinance — A refinance originated by Unlock. Settlement fee is waived.
- Underpayment — When customer wires less than the full settlement amount.
- Right of Rescission — Homeowner cancels within the legal rescission window.


STANDARD SETTLEMENT PROCESSING
--------------------------------
Trigger: Notification appears in #settlement_queue Slack channel when a wire is processed.

1. Monitor #settlement_queue for new settlement notifications.

2. Identify required inputs:
   - Asset ID (if missing, use Badger to locate via other identifiers such as name, address,
     file number).
   - Wire Amount.

3. Open Settlement Quick Publisher. Click Paintbrush Button to clear prior data.
   File path: Asset Management - Documents\05 - Initiatives\Settlement Quick Publisher\
   SettlementQuickPublish - JPM_Formatted.xlsm

4. Enter settlement data:
   - Verify closing date is accurate.
   - Enter Asset ID. Name, address, and original investment amount auto-populate in ~10–15 sec.
   - Enter Wire Amount.
   - Click Wire Transfer Button. Remaining fields auto-populate.
   - Note: Settlement fee = $500 (waived for Unlock Refinances). Lien release fee = $75.

5. Handle atypical cases:
   - If any populated columns look off, investigate and/or escalate before proceeding.

6. Click Email Man Button:
   - Drafts email to Suntera for transfer/refund.
   - Updates MAX database.
   - Excel pop-up confirms success.

7. Slack notification:
   - Auto-navigated to the Wire-Trades tab. Copy the message and post to #settlements channel.
   - React to the original message in #settlement_queue to confirm processing.

8. Refunds:
   - Refunds populate in a separate spreadsheet used by Accounting.
   - Confirm that information has been populated. Entry point:
     ProcessOldestIncomingSettlementWire (excludes the latest settlement in the queue).


CUSTOMER REFUND FLOW OF FUNDS
------------------------------
When a customer's settlement results in an overpayment or refund:
1. Settlement funds wire from customer to TCB Omnibus Collections (x3844).
2. Within 2 BD: InvOps sweeps exercise value + settlement fee + lien release fee to
   investor trust holdings account. (These fees are recouped via remittance at month end.)
3. The overpayment amount is moved to UAM TCB account; simultaneously, the same amount is
   transferred from the pre-funded JPM UAM account to JPM Omnibus Collections.
4. Refund check is sent from JPM Omnibus account to customer.
5. JPM UAM account is periodically pre-funded to maintain balance for sending refunds.


UNDERPAYMENT PROCESSING
------------------------
Overview: If a regular underpayment occurs, notify Christine D and hold off on processing
the settlement until the underpayment is received. Once full amount received, manually
add both transactions to Badger.

1. Process the settlement using the FULL amount (initial wire + underpayment).
   Enter the sum into the Wire Amount Overwrite field on the Settlement Quick Publisher.
   The Settlement Calculator will flag underpayments; do not process until actual
   underpayment is received.

2. After settlement is processed, retrieve the TCB Bank Trans IDs using:
   EXEC GetTCBBankTrans [amount1]
   EXEC GetTCBBankTrans [amount2]
   (Run one for the original wire amount and one for the underpayment amount.)

3. Include the BANKTRANS_ID in the first column of the Month-of Settlement processed tab
   in the Settlement Quick Publisher.

4. Make Badger entry:
   - Search settlement in Badger → navigate to Settlement tab.
   - Select Cash Transactions tab → Edit Grid → Paste BANKTRANS_ID.
   - Transaction Type = Incoming Wire.
   - Note = "Underpayment received".

5. Post message in #refunds Slack channel:
   "Underpayment Received - Asset ID: [id] - Name: [name] - Amount: $[amount]"

Unlock Refinance underpayment: Waive underpayment — inform Servicing, get OK to waive.
Move money to Collections to cover underpayment; instruct Accounting to transfer.

All Other Settlement Types: Do not process until all funds received. If approval from
Servicing to waive underpayment, same step as Unlock Refi — move money to Collections.


SETTLEMENTS WITHOUT COLLECTIONS MAPPING
-----------------------------------------
Overview: Periodic process to map outgoing BANKTRANS IDs for settlements that do not yet
have corresponding Collections transfers recorded.

Trigger: Monitor Badger for email with subject "Settlements without Collections Transfers".

Steps:
1. From the spreadsheet attached to the email, select columns L–O (including headers).
2. Use =SQL.Insert("SettlementTrans",) in Excel to generate insert statements.
3. Navigate to the generated file path and execute the SQL.
4. After DB is updated, update the Settlement Quick Publisher with the outgoing BANKTRANS IDs
   from the Collections Transfer file using a VLOOKUP.
   Outgoing banktrans_ids are mapped in the corresponding Month-of tab.


DELETING / CANCELLING A SETTLEMENT
------------------------------------
Use this stored procedure to delete a previously processed settlement:
  EXEC dbo.ResetSettlement '[asset_id]'

After running the stored proc:
1. Delete the record from the Month-of tab in the Settlement Quick Publisher.
2. Delete from the Tracker sheet.
3. Confirm in #Badger_Ops Slack channel — state that stored proc was used and why.


SETTLEMENTS JOURNALING
-----------------------
Overview: Journal entries for closed settlements must be inserted into dbo.Trades.
Badger sends a daily email (subject: "Assets Needing Close Out Journal Entries") with an
attached Excel file for any assets requiring close-out journal entries. This email recurs
daily for the prior month's settlements until all are cleared.

Steps:
1. Find email with subject "Assets Needing Close Out Journal Entries".
2. Open the attached .xls file.
3. Change cell K2 to General type (otherwise the SQL.Insert function will not work).
4. In cell K2, enter:
   =SQL.Insert("Trades", L1:S2)
   where the range must include the header row through the full data range.
   This uses the UnlockToolkit function to generate INSERT statements for dbo.Trades.
   If there are enough records, it will output them to a file and show the file path.

   Example output:
   insert into Trades ([ASSET_ID],[OFACE],[ACCOUNT_ID],[BUY_SELL],[COUNTERPARTY_ID],
   [TRADE_DATE],[SETTLE_DATE],[PRICE]) values ('10957325119729',55808,16,'S',0,
   '12/15/2024 12:00:00 AM','11/30/2024 12:00:00 AM',139.642864822248)

5. Put the insert statements in a SQL client and execute against the UAM database.

REO SALE PROCESSING
--------------------
REO (Real Estate Owned) sales occur when an asset is auctioned following foreclosure.
Settlement proceeds from the REO sale are posted to the x8972 Collections account at TCB.

1. Confirm funds have hit the x8972 Collections account in TCB.

2. Email Billing and Daisy requesting them to move funds from x8972 Collections into the
   appropriate investor account. Include a screenshot of the settlement receipt notification.

3. Update the REO table using the following SQL (substitute actual asset_id and values):

   UPDATE REO
   SET reo_settlement_date = 'YYYY-MM-DD'
   WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'

   UPDATE REO
   SET reo_net_proceeds = [net_proceeds_amount]
   WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'

   UPDATE REO
   SET reo_home_sale_price = [home_sale_price]
   WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'

   Field mappings from the Asset Auctioned V.2 Tracker:
   - reo_settlement_date = REO Dispo/Settlement Date
   - reo_net_proceeds = REO Settlement Amount (Net Proceeds)
   - reo_home_sale_price = REO Home Sale Price

4. Inform Justin Burke.


FUNDED ASSET CANCELLATION
--------------------------
Purpose: Process a cancellation for an asset that was funded but must be reversed.
Triggered by post-closing requesting cancellation. Funds must be wired back to
UF2 Collections account (x3844) by title company or customer.

Wire return instructions (for communicating to title/customer):
  Bank: Texas Capital Bank
  ABA: 111017979
  Account Number: 2400053844
  Account Name: UF2 Collections Account

Step 1: Check if asset has been traded.
  SELECT * FROM dbo.Trades WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'
  If traded to a trust, STOP. Confirm next steps with team in #ninja_life.

Step 2: Run stored procedure (handles most steps automatically).
  Standard cancellation:
    EXEC ProcessAssetFailedToClose '[asset_id]'
  Right of Rescission (sets uwissue_id = 6):
    EXEC ProcessAssetFailedToClose '[asset_id]', 1

Step 3: Notify counterparties.
  - Email TCB at HEA_Loans@texascapitalbank.com — confirm funds received back to x3844,
    request removal from the warehouse line. Include asset ID, address, amount, reason.
  - Forward to Suntera (unlock@suntera.com, cc billing@unlock.com).
  - Forward to WSFS (custodygroup@wsfsbank.com).
  - Post confirmation in #Ninja-Life and #Post-Closing Slack channels.

Manual steps if stored proc fails:
  1. Mark Fundings_New status as Cancelled (fundingstatus_id = 4):
     UPDATE Fundings_New SET fundingstatus_id = 4 WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'

  2. Delete journal entries from Trades:
     DELETE FROM Trades WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'

  3. Set Underwriting Issue:
     Standard Failed to Close (uwissue_id = 5):
       UPDATE Underwriting SET uwissue_id = 5 WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'
     Right of Rescission (uwissue_id = 6):
       UPDATE Underwriting SET uwissue_id = 6 WHERE asset_id = '[asset_id]'


MARGIN CALL PROCESSING
-----------------------
TCB sends an email notification when a margin call is triggered.
Margin calls occur when an asset has been on the warehouse line for >30 days,
then again at day 45 and day 60.

Steps:
1. Verify the call amount against the collateral tab.
2. Log into TCB → Activity Center → Funds Transfer.
   Transfer the required amount from UPS (x0292) to UF2 Collections (x3844).
3. Inform TCB that the amount has been transferred.
